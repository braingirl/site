10 Things That Made My Home Feel Like a Sanctuary

There's a version of home styling that requires a full renovation budget, a designer, and a complete lifestyle overhaul. This is not that post. What I want to share are the small, considered decisions — some under $20 — that quietly shifted the feeling of my home from fine to genuinely restorative.

The things that actually moved the needle

I've moved a lot. Seven apartments in ten years. And somewhere along the way I started paying attention to what made certain spaces feel calming versus chaotic — not just the big things, but the specifics. The weight of a throw blanket. The height of a lamp. Whether there was one clear surface to rest your eyes on.

01 — linen everything

I replaced my cotton bedding with linen duvet covers two years ago and haven't looked back. The texture makes a bed look lived-in and beautiful rather than sterile. The wrinkles are the point.

02 — one great candle

I was skeptical of the "candle as lifestyle" thing until I found one that genuinely changed the atmosphere of a room. It's not about the scent being expensive — it's about it being consistent. Your home starts to smell like itself.

I've been burning this one for over a year and reorder it without thinking. Cedar, a little smoke, something vaguely botanical. It works in every room.

03 — edit, don't add

The single most impactful thing I did was remove things. Took three boxes to a donation center and immediately felt the difference. Breathing room is underrated. The eye needs somewhere to land and rest.

A few more worth mentioning

  • A waffle-weave throw draped over the arm of a chair — instantly inviting
  • Decanting pantry staples into glass jars (a Sunday afternoon project that you'll never regret)
  • Switching to warm-toned bulbs throughout — overhead lighting at 2700K is everything
  • One architectural plant (a fiddle leaf or olive tree) does more than five small ones
  • Linen napkins that actually get used, not stored

None of these are revolutionary. But that's the point. The home that feels like a sanctuary isn't the one that was professionally styled — it's the one that was paid attention to.

If you try any of these, I'd love to hear what shifts for you.
